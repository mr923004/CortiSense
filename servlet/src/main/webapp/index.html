About your readings
Cortisol is a hormone your body produces naturally throughout the day. It's highest in the morning — this helps you wake up and feel energised — then gradually falls through the afternoon and evening, reaching its lowest point at night. Short spikes are completely normal and are often caused by stress, exercise, or even a strong coffee. Your readings are measured in nanomoles per litre (nmol/L). This app is a research demonstration — always speak to your GP if you have concerns about your health.
